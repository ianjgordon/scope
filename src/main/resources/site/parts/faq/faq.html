About Mindful Monsters

What is Mindful Monsters?

Mindful Monsters is a new, fun way to support the charity Scope. Each month, you donate £7.50 to Scope and, in return, you get seven exciting family activity cards inspired by mindfulness.

Mindful Monsters gives your kids essential life skills and your donations support parents to give their disabled children the best start in life - it’s a win/win.

There are 84 cards to collect in total, over 12 months. And once your collection is complete, we hope you will continue to support disabled people and their families through your ongoing donation to Scope.

Why should I use Mindful Monsters?

Being a parent puts a lot of pressure on your time, there's no denying that! So, what little quality time you can spend with your kids needs to be special and memorable.

That's why parents love Mindful Monsters – you'll find activities that can create belly-busting laughter or diffuse tearful tantrums and turn potential meltdown triggers - like getting ready for school or going to bed - into magic moments together.

And, best of all, each activity takes as little or as long as you like and can fit into the busiest of days.

What is mindfulness?

It's slowly taking over school, parenting blogs and even your Facebook feed, but what exactly is mindfulness?

Mindfulness is simply the art of being in the present - paying attention to your thoughts and feelings, moment by moment.

Many people have found that when we pause and soak up the present, we improve our focus, achieve a sense of calm and have a better overall sense of wellbeing.

Mindful Monsters activities are inspired by this principle, and help children develop emotional resilience and important life skills.

Is there any proof that mindfulness works?

Lots! There have been many studies that show that practicing mindfulness creates physical changes in our brains which, to put it simply, make us happier.

And that’s not all - scientists have seen that these changes last long after the mindfulness activities are over, as long as we are regularly mindful.

Here's what Michael Chaskalson, a Leading Mindfulness Expert, has to say about mindfulness:

"Mindfulness changes lives. Kids who can manage their own emotions do better and an early introduction to mindfulness can really help. When kids learn mindfulness, it sustains their natural curiosity, helping them to engage more deeply with others and with the world around them."

If you choose to donate monthly, your gifts will be taken on the 1st of every month. If you choose to give annually, your first gift will be taken on the 1st of next month, and continue to be taken on the same date every year.

What is gift aid?

Gift aid is a fantastic governmental scheme, which means that if you are a tax payer, charities may be able to claim an additional 25% of your donation amount from the government.

To claim gift aid on any eligible donations to Scope, make sure you tick the gift aid box at checkout. It will mean your donations to Scope will go even further to support disabled children and their families.

How will my donations be spent?

Your donations will help support families to build resilience in their day-to-day lives and give their disabled children the best start in life. For every pound we spend:

  • 70p goes towards supporting disabled people and their families.
  • 20p is spent on selling your donated goods through our shops, which raises even more money for us.
  • 9p is spent on fundraising with our supporters (like you).
  • 1p is spent on making sure the charity is efficient and well run.

Who is Scope?

Scope is a charity that provides support, information and advice to more than a quarter of a million disabled people and their families every year. We raise awareness of the barriers facing disabled people and, with your support, we help them build resilience in their day-to-day lives.
